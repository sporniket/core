# Contributing policies

> As a Free and Open Source Software author, I WON'T include nor accept code generated by a LLM into the codebase, in order to keep the code protected by copyright. 
> 
> **Rationale** : DC Circuit’s March 2025 decision in Thaler v. Perlmutter, confirmed that AI-generated works are ineligible for copyright protection.

This project primarily address my own needs. As such, I WILL likely decline any feature request (but thank you for your suggesting). In this case, just fork it, change the maven groupId to your own, implement your feature, document your own contribution, and publish it under your name. That’s the freedom granted to you by the *GPL.

I MAY accept bug reports and PR of fixes (only human written code, please see above my stance about AI-generated code), but I can take time to finally be published. I mean, you may push your feedback during a period where I do something else, like getting a life or working on other projects. Thank you nonetheless for the effort.

And anyway, by the virtue of the open source licence I am using, you may just fork the project, change it to your needs, and publish it with proper attribution.

Have a nice day.
